# SOP (Niyet Mektubu) Yapısı — Admit-lab Kaynaklı

Kaynak: Dr. Philippe Barr, "Statement of Purpose for Psychology: What Committees Want", The Admit Lab.

## Komitenin Baktığı 4 Sinyal

Committee members evaluate SOPs strategically. They're looking for:

### 1. Clear Intellectual Direction
Programs want: what area you're most interested in and why.
- Clinical, counseling, developmental, cognitive, I/O
- Risk: uncertainty about direction weakens program fit.

### 2. Meaningful Engagement with Psychology (with Reflection)
Evidence: research assistantships, lab work, internships, volunteer work, thesis.
- Key: NOT just participation — reflection on what you LEARNED.

### 3. Realistic Goals and Pathway Understanding
Clarify: licensure as therapist? Research? Consulting? PhD?
- Goal clarity helps committees evaluate fit.

### 4. Understanding the Specific Program
Show familiarity with: practicum opportunities, research labs, faculty interests.
- Risk: generic essays that could be sent anywhere → WEAKEN application.

## SOP Yapısı (5 Paragraf)

### 1. Opening: Intellectual Motivation
- How your interest developed
- Specific experiences/observations, NOT abstract "help people" statements

### 2. Academic Preparation
- Psychology research methods, thesis, lab experience, statistical training
- NOT just course listing — what did you LEARN?

### 3. Professional/Applied Experience
- Mental health volunteering, RA roles, internships, community work
- Emphasis: "real-world grounding"

### 4. Career Goals
- What do you want graduate training to enable?
- What kind of psychologist do you want to become?

### 5. Program Fit (Why THIS Program)
- Specific faculty, labs, practicum sites, training model
- Avoid: generic praise ("excellent university", "world-class faculty")

## Ek Öğrenilenler

- GPA zayıfsa SOP'ta savunma — komite transkripti zaten görür. Deneyimlere odaklan.
- Kurumsal iddiaları (staj, lab, hoca) MUTLAKA doğrula. Doğrulanamayan → çıkar.
- Türkçe SOP'ta AI pattern'leri İngilizce'den farklı çalışır (em dash az, "düşünüyorum" normal).
